ProveIt is a decentralized application (ƒêApp for short) powered by Ethereum. It offers users a simple value proposition: the ability to prove that they said or possessed something, anything, without the need to rely on a trusted intermediary. This is an extraordinary and unprecedented phenomenon with implications for intellectual property rights, credibility in peer-to-peer networks, and much more.

Using ProveIt is simple: first, decide what text, file, or arbitrary data you want to be able to prove that you possessed. Since ProveIt never stores unencrypted data, we first have to compute a unique identifier for your submission: its Keccak-256 hash. Using Keccak ensures that the content of your submission remains anonymous while still allowing anyone to verify it. If this is confusing, don't worry: any text or file you submit to ProveIt will automatically be processed with secure, client-side hashing. Of course, you're always welcome to compute and submit your own hash. This identifier is then submitted to ProveIt alongside an (optional) amount of ether that you want to lock up to provide credibility. This amount cannot be recovered without destroying the associated submission. Once your submission has been made, it's immutably stored in the blockchain! At any point in the future, you can release the original text, file, or data at your discretion, allowing anyone to verify that you possessed it.

More information on ProveIt (including hypothetical use cases), and the entirety of the open-source codebase can be found on Github.
